What Job Best Fits Your Life?

When we talk about jobs, we often talk about “fit.” Most commonly, fit refers to how well a candidate matches up with a company and the role. One thing that sometimes gets overlooked, though, is how a job fits into your life. Job success is based on both of these factors, so knowing what you’re looking for before getting into the job market can save you significant time and energy. So how do you get there?
Taking into account factors like your biggest motivators, how much you want to work, and how you spend your time outside the office can give you important clues to what kind of position will fit most seamlessly into your life. Take our quiz to find out your best fit.

5 tips to always be ready for job opportunities

Refresh your resume There’s a reason why people have separate resumes for different types of jobs. No two jobs are alike, and your resumes shouldn’t be either. “There is no one-size-fits-all for resumes, so each diverse and intriguing skill you can add to your qualifications should be noted,” says Jennifer Lee Magas, vice president of Magas Media Consultants, LLC, a public relations firm in Connecticut. “Each job is going to have different requirements.”

Document your awesomeness This will allow you to track your wins as you go,” says Angela Copeland, career coach at Copeland Coaching in Memphis, Tennessee. “It will also keep the pressure low if someone like a recruiter or hiring manager at another company asks to see your portfolio when you aren't thinking about a new job.”

Get smart(er) on your own time And if your current company doesn’t offer professional development? Don’t fret: There are many affordable or free learning opportunities that will help you add skills to your resume and keep your ability to passively job search primed. A few old faithfuls? YouTube, Udemy, and Coursera.

Polish your professional development “You should first investigate the training and development offerings at your company,” says John DiMarco, communications professor at St. John's University in New York City, and author of Career Power Skills. “I advise my students to go to any conference, tradeshow, or training class that they can.”

Double down on job alerts If you’re in a sweet position that you like just fine, fine-tune your job alerts to align with your ultimate dream jobs. That way, your alerts feature jobs that are your absolute top choices.

Key Takeaways

1. Employment is a paid work agreement between an employer and an employee.
2. An employment agreement for an individual employee can be a verbal exchange, written email, or job offer letter.
3. Employment agreements vary and may involve different time commitments and compensation plans.
4. Federal, state, and local laws also impact employment.
